# Cepheid Pricing Strategy & Margin Analysis

Granola meeting: `9ba134d9-9f43-4c7f-b55b-07f5de92ec3a`

## Summary

McKinsey identified $46–75M margin opportunity for Cepheid over 2 years. $11M achievable in 2026, ramping to $152–226M cumulative by 2029. Focus on pricing excellence — NOT raising 4Plex prices (confirmed multiple times).

## CFO Concern (Charles Mwangi)

Won't underwrite until he sees breakdown of pure pricing vs volume/menu expansion. Needs apples-to-apples with existing forecast bridge. McKinsey agreed to provide by Friday for FC5.

## Major Levers ($46–75M)

- **Product packages** (1A–C): $13–25M — promotional packages with stipulated mix
- **Pricing architecture** (3A–B): $9–13M — mitigate RRA erosion, non-4Plex realization
- **Deal governance** (4A–D): $6–9M — micro-segmentation, move bottom quintile up

## Competitive Context

- Abbott: 87% platform consolidation vs Cepheid's 38%
- Survey (n=48): service reliability is Cepheid's differentiator; cost per test is weakness
- Customers prefer broader menu over lower unit price

## Implementation

- Needs 2–3 analytical roles (pricing analytics, CPQ owner, contract compliance)
- Cepheid already piloting pricing tool (launched March 2026)
- Mini-pilot approach before full rollout
