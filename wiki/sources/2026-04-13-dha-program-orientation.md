# DHA Program Orientation — Course Planning with Admissions Advisor

Madina met with her OSU admissions advisor to review her course plan for the Doctor of Healthcare Administration program.

## Course Plan Review

### Fall 2026 (First Semester)
- **5123 Research & Evaluation** — 16-week format (advisor confirmed this is the right call; professor prefers 16-week for the semester-long research project)
- Two 8-week courses paired alongside it
- Advisor confirmed the schedule looks good — Dina is "well ahead of everybody else"

### Spring & Beyond
- Advisor reviewed and approved the full multi-semester plan
- Seminars spread across semesters (advisor liked this approach)
- Summer courses with Dr. Shay (8-week format)
- All 6000-level classes offered every fall and spring — no rotation concerns

### Key Faculty
- **Dr. Keatoner** — teaches 5123 Research; also teaches across forensic sciences and biomedical sciences programs
- **Professor Hunter** — healthcare law; works in compliance office for OSU medical center
- **Dr. Shay** — innovation, rural health, AI in medical school support
- **Dr. Vargas** — former administrator at St. Francis Health System (Tulsa's largest hospital)

## Program Logistics

### Orientation
- Week before classes start in August
- Two sessions (lunchtime + evening); also recorded
- Faculty will introduce themselves

### Platform & Communication
- **Canvas** — primary LMS; courses appear grayed out until ~1 week before start
- **DHA Canvas page** — separate resource hub with syllabi, discussion board, helpful resources
- **Monday Memo** — weekly email from graduate college (mostly for main campus; DHA-specific comms come from advisor)
- Advisor limits mass emails to 1-2 per semester at deadline times
- LinkedIn group, Facebook group, Reddit page exist (student-run, advisor stays out intentionally)

### Financial
- Bursar bill posts mid-July
- Payment plan or financial aid due by September 15
- No payment required before classes start

### Spring Enrollment
- Opens ~3rd week of October
- Schedule available early October; advisor sends email

## Dina's Positioning
- Pitched herself as product management / technology background, always in healthcare space
- Currently at a medical device company
- Taking DHA to understand hospital decision-making (her products go to those settings)
- Was nervous about being from a tech background — advisor assured diverse backgrounds are normal (nurses, CEOs, CFOs, engineers, military medical corps, tribal health, rural health, VA)
- Most common degrees coming in: MHA and MBA

## Key Takeaway
- Advisor: "Take advantage of your peers" — learning from classmates' diverse experiences is as valuable as coursework
- Discussion posts are a networking opportunity, not just an assignment
