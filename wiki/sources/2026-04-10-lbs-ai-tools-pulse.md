# LBS AI Tools Adoption Pulse — Interactive Session

Madina ran an interactive polling/pulse session with the LBS San Diego team using a custom app she built. This was part of the on-site training week.

## Pulse Results

### AI Tool Usage Baseline
- Most attendees use AI tools at least occasionally
- No one reported "not at all" — baseline is better than expected
- Mix of experimental and regular usage

### Key Pain Points Surfaced
- **Documentation** — came up repeatedly as the biggest time sink; AI could help generate release notes, test docs, procedure docs
- **Unit testing** — developers skip it due to time pressure; AI could generate tests
- **Handoff from dev to QA** — process is broken; people can't find information they used to find easily
- **Permission barriers** — developers can solve problems with AI but can't execute solutions due to enterprise permission restrictions
- **GitHub Copilot mismatch** — not all teams use GitHub; forcing Copilot on non-GitHub teams creates waste ("muda")

### Developer Feedback Themes
- "Meet us where we are" — developers want tools validated for their specific environments, not one-size-fits-all
- Some teams (Melbourne, Shanghai) are already advanced and self-organizing
- Interest in building custom agents for workspace management and status reporting
- Cross-functional documentation and knowledge management is a real gap

### Cursor Pilot
- LBS has several Cursor licenses through the pilot program
- Ty (AI champion for LBS) manages license distribution
- $100/month token allowance per license, covered by Enterprise AI team
- Anish and Keith are active users — triggered Madina's VOC interest
- Anish is #4 on the Danaher Cursor leaderboard (last 30 days)

### Madina's Observations
- Built a custom pulse/presentation app for interactive feedback (voting, real-time results)
- Positioned herself as EAI resource: "I used to be part of LBS — can't get rid of me"
- Selfishly motivated — wants to see this team succeed
- Heard clear signal that training approach needs rethinking
