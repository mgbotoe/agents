# Value Scoring — Ops Impact First

## Decision
Reorder the strategic value scorer: V1 is now **Ops Impact** (was Member Impact). V2 is now **Audience & Impact** (was Ops Impact, reframed from "member impact" to broader lens).

## What Helen Said
> "I think that strategic value to internal operations first versus member value will be higher ROI. I started this conversation thinking it was flipped, but actually I think that most of the value right now is helping us really operate AI natively. The member stuff — we built the portal, it's good enough."

> "The ops stuff is the hard stuff in between, and that's where value in providing judgment and scope becomes really powerful."

## Additional Refinements from This Call

1. **"Audience and impact" not "member impact"** — V1/V2 reframing to be broader than just members
2. **Graduation model** — projects progress standalone → lightly integrated → deeply integrated naturally. Framework should support re-classification.
3. **Access level classifier** — add read-only vs read/write as a spectrum modifier (Q4 in classifier)
4. **Cut test** — "Anything on the list means you'd have eyes on it. Do you want eyes on so many things? If not, how do you cut?"
5. **Gumloop risk** — scanning Slack errs toward "interesting ideas" not "valuable to us." Strategic value filter must be primary gate.
6. **Roadmap visibility** — framework output needs to read as a roadmap, not just a classification matrix
7. **Core team VOC** — present framework to sub-teams to validate prioritization signals
